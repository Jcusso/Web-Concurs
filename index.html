Technical Specification of the Competition

Introduction

The World Bridge Design Competition is a global initiative aimed at encouraging innovation in the design of vital infrastructure for connectivity and urban development. Engineers, architects, designers, and students from around the world are invited to submit their bridge design proposals, focused on improving the transport connections of goods and passengers in selected cities.

Objectives

  • Promote innovation and creativity in bridge design.
  • Improve urban infrastructure through efficient and sustainable engineering solutions.
  • Facilitate the exchange of knowledge and experiences among professionals and students from various disciplines.
  • Foster the development of projects that can be implemented globally to improve the quality of life and economy in cities.
In collaboration with: International Association For Bridge And Structural Engineering

Project Requirements

Bridge Design

  • The design must be innovative, functional, and sustainable.
  • It must include detailed plans and a physical scale model.
  • Include technical specifications such as dimensions, materials, and proposed construction methods.
  • The design must comply with international construction and safety standards.

Proposed Location

  • Select a city where the construction of the bridge would be significantly beneficial.
  • Justify the choice of location with traffic analysis, environmental and social impact studies, and other relevant data.
  • Provide an implementation plan detailing the positive impact on the city, including improvements in mobility and the transport of goods and passengers.

Budget

  • Present a detailed budget including costs of materials, labor, permits, preliminary studies, and any other associated expenses.
  • Include an economic and financial feasibility analysis of the project.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Innovation and Creativity (25%): Originality of the design and innovative use of materials and techniques.
  • Sustainability and Efficiency (20%): Use of sustainable materials and energy efficiency of the design.
  • Functionality and Viability (20%): Structural efficiency of the bridge and feasibility of its construction.
  • Justification of the Location (15%): Quality and solidity of the arguments presented to justify the choice of the city.
  • Social and Environmental Impact (10%): Projected benefits for the community and the environment.
  • Budget and Financial Viability (10%): Realism and detail of the budget, as well as the economic viability of the project.

Prizes

  • First Prize: Full funding for the construction of the bridge at the proposed location, along with international recognition.
  • Second Prize: Partial funding and a special recognition.
  • Third Prize: Special recognition and a certificate of participation.
